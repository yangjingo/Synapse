- It is one of the clearest examples of diagram-first technical explanation.
- The prose and figures are tightly interlocked.
- The diagrams make abstract mechanism intuitive without losing rigor.

## Writing Traits

- title style: concept-explainer headline
- section rhythm: intuition -> diagram -> local explanation -> next refinement
- paragraph density: moderate
- caption style: short and supportive
- typography impression: quiet long-form reading experience

## Figure Traits

- figure classes used:
  - unrolled sequence diagrams
  - component decomposition diagrams
  - step-by-step mechanism visuals
  - variant comparison visuals
- color behavior:
  - soft but semantic color coding
  - colors help distinguish operations and states
- shape and line language:
  - highly recognizable schematic language
  - repeated visual grammar across the article
- label style:
  - minimal and pedagogical
- density:
  - medium
- chart style:
  - mostly schematic rather than benchmark-heavy

## Reusable Rules

- Repeat a stable visual grammar across the whole article.
- Build understanding by refining one recurring base diagram.
- Pair each conceptual step with a figure that isolates only that step.

## Avoid

- overloading a single diagram with all details at once
- changing visual grammar too often within one article
