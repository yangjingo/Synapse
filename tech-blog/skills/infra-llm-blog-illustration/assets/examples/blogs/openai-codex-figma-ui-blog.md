## Why It Is Good

- It is operational and easy to follow.
- The design feels clean, intentional, and product-grade.
- It uses media to support workflow explanation without making the article feel overloaded.

## Writing Traits

- title style: clear task-oriented how-to headline
- section rhythm: use case -> step sequence -> tool explanation -> workflow continuation
- paragraph density: lower than research-style writing, but still information-rich
- caption style: minimal; much of the visual explanation is embedded in media
- typography impression: polished product-doc style with strong hierarchy

## Figure Traits

- figure classes used:
  - hero image
  - product workflow visuals
  - embedded motion demos / video
- color behavior:
  - polished brand-consistent palette
  - clean neutrals with controlled accents
- shape and line language:
  - product UI surfaces
  - clear framing and spacing
- label style:
  - concise and operational
- density:
  - moderate
- motion style:
  - workflow demonstration rather than abstract animation

## Reusable Rules

- For workflow articles, use visuals to reduce procedural ambiguity.
- Keep the page visually clean so operational steps remain primary.
- Use strong spacing and hierarchy to support fast scanning.

## Avoid

- turning deep technical mechanism articles into product-marketing layouts
- replacing explicit diagrams with screenshots when diagrams would teach better
